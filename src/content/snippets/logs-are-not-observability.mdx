---
title: "Logs Are Not Observability"
description: "Logs are data. Observability is the ability to understand your system from that data."
date: "2026-06-20"
tags: ["observability"]
status: "published"
---

I see many teams conflate logging with observability.

They ship logs to a service, create a few dashboards, and declare victory. But that's not observability. That's just... data storage.

Observability is the ability to ask arbitrary questions about your system's behavior without having instrumented for them in advance.

Logs alone don't give you that. You need logs *plus* structured fields, *plus* the ability to filter and correlate, *plus* someone who knows how to ask the right questions.

Most teams stop after shipping logs.
